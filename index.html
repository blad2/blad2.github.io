Some of the important dates of the inventions and discoveries related to time:

  • 3982 B.C. – Artifacts from the Paleolithic suggest the use of lunar calendar 12-13 lunar months
  • 1525 B.C. – The Water Clock or Clepsydra is predicted to be the most precise timekeeper at the time, this could be used even at night time.
  • 1500 B.C. – Egyptian created the first devices able to measure the passage of time
  • 350 B.C. – There is the first representation of the hourglass
  • 45 B.C. – Julius Caesar sets Rome on a Solar system calendar
  • 1000. – Chinese inventors and engineers invented the mechanical clock by escapment mechanism
  • 1582. -Pope Gregory XIII Introduces the correction of the solar calendar; Gregorian Calendar
  • 1293. – Richard of Wallingford built the mechanical clock
  • 1847. – Greenwich Mean Time (GMT), created in UK for the rail system, navy and shipping industry.
  • 1884. – 41 nations agreed and signed a collaboration of the development of The Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) or Universal Time Coordinated
  • 1851. – Physics started to look closer to the Spacetime as something related to what we call time.
